Last Day with Diesel

March 7, 2020

Categories: Art Poetry

   

I wrote this after the death of our beloved Great Dane named Diesel.

   

Last Day with Diesel

free of collar and of leash he paused to keep the pace,
we watched with apprehension fearing pain upon his face

he stood and looked around him that gray and misty day,
in a field, his domain, that had just been cleared of hay

he paused for a moment, while the breeze brought him a smell,
only visible to us as the leaves slowly fell

little hurried birds swirled and sang close by,
perhaps those tiny angels were his link to the sky

there was a spring in his step that filled our hearts with glee,
but that was our last time with him, our favorite company

and the mist gave way to rain and the gray became darkness

we expect to find you in a room, but you are not around,
we listen for your collar noise, but there is not a sound

we can still feel your fur, so soft along your ears,
we relive what we shared with you these past nine years

we assume you are just beyond the hedge row, along the boundary line,
this time you are in the lead, ahead of me and mine

Casey J. Davis
2015.09.20
